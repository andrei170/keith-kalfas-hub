Funnel state

Top of funnel is exceptional. The YouTube long-form catalogue has 10+ years of evergreen "how to start a landscaping business" content, the podcast is on every major platform, the IG/TikTok cadence is consistent, the brand language ("The Landscaping Employee Trap") is owned in the niche.

Email capture exists (Free Mini Marketing course + 7 Steps PDF + Brand Advantage Workshop) but it is PDF-shaped, not scorecard-shaped. A landscaper who finishes a Keith YouTube video does not get diagnosed - they get a PDF. There is no segmentation between "year one solo with no clients" / "year three with one truck stuck at $80K rev" / "year five with three trucks burning out trying to scale." All three buy a different next product. The current capture treats them as one segment.

Mid funnel is courses. The $97-$347 range converts the cold viewer into a buyer well, but it is the wrong shape for an operator who already crossed $100K revenue and needs implementation help, not another module library. The Alliance ($97/mo) is the only recurring layer and it is positioned more as community-plus-Q&A than as a structured outcome. An operator who wants "by week 4 my pricing is fixed, by week 8 I have hired my first crew lead" has no product to buy.

High funnel is the 1:1 coaching call. Price hidden behind the click. No public group cohort, no done-with-you operator package, no $2K-$5K mid-tier structured mentorship between the Alliance and the 1:1 call. Every dollar above $347 has to route through Keith's calendar one client at a time.

Cross-sell between the affiliate stack (Jobber, CallRail, etc.) and the personal brand is mature - this is one of the strongest pieces of the funnel and does not need rebuilding. The new mid-tier layer should compound this, not compete with it.

Three things I would build differently

#1 - The mid-tier cohort between the Alliance and the 1:1 coaching call

The asymmetry: Free content is excellent. The Alliance is $97/mo and does the community job. The 1:1 coaching call is the only thing above $347 and it is bottlenecked by Keith's hours. There is no $2K-$5K structured group product for the operator who is past free content, past the $347 bundle, ready to do real implementation work, but not yet ready for ongoing 1:1.

A "Landscaping Operator Cohort" - 8 weeks, weekly group call with Keith, scheduled office hours, 20-25 seat cap - built around four installs that an operator can sign their name to:

  • Pricing fixed (week 1-2) - the bid script, the per-property pricing matrix, the upsell ladder, raised on the next 3 estimates
  • First crew lead hired (week 3-4) - the job ad, the interview script, the pay structure, the 30-60-90 onboarding
  • Route economics locked (week 5-6) - the route map, the windshield-time-per-dollar number, the de-route plan
  • Owner-out-of-the-truck plan (week 7-8) - the 12-month sequence to remove the owner from production, with the dollar checkpoint at each stage

Year-1 floor: 4-6 cohorts per year x 20-25 seats x $2K-$5K = $160K-$750K direct revenue on the cohort line alone, before Alliance retention lift or 1:1 spillover.

#2 - 36K IG plus a podcast catalogue and no scorecard capturing email

The audience watches a Reel about pricing, taps the bio link, lands on either the Alliance signup, the bundle, or a free PDF. There is no diagnostic that returns a specific result. A landscaper who is bidding $50/cut and a landscaper who runs 3 trucks at $1M/year both get the same PDF.

A "Landscaping Business Trap Score" - 12 questions, returns a 0-100 score plus a 3-line bottleneck readout (which trap is currently costing you the most: pricing, hiring, route economics, or owner-as-bottleneck) - segments the inbound subscriber on capture, then routes them to the right product (Alliance for community-stage, bundle for content-stage, cohort for implementation-stage, 1:1 call for top-stage).

5-8% IG-to-email is realistic on a high-intent trades audience with the right diagnostic = 2K-3K incremental subscribers in 90 days from the existing IG base alone, more once it goes on the podcast + YouTube end cards. Year-1 lift: $60K-$160K from scorecard-segmented sales across the existing product ladder, compounds for life of the list.

#3 - The Untrapped Podcast catalogue has no productised cohort waitlist or end-card upsell

The podcast is on every platform and has hundreds of episodes. Each episode currently ends in either no CTA, a generic "go to keithkalfas.com," or an affiliate plug. The catalogue is the warmest possible audience for a $2K-$5K cohort - they have already listened to 30-60 minutes of Keith in a single sitting. They are not casual scrollers.

A "Cohort Waitlist" page (one-pager with the cohort overview, application form, cohort cycle dates, what gets installed by week 4 vs week 8) ships with one job: the podcast end-card, the YouTube end-card, and the podcast show notes all route here. The Alliance stays as the recurring layer; the cohort waitlist is the implementation upgrade for listeners who want to do real work.

Year-1 floor: $80K-$200K incremental from podcast-attributable cohort applications + waitlist conversion on the existing catalogue. Compounds with every new episode shipped.

Sensitivities

  • Keith is the named operator in this niche. No need to build credibility. The pitch lands the unit economics on the missing mid-tier layer, not the brand authority.
  • Trades register. Keith's voice is direct, plain, working-truck. Avoid finance-bro jargon, agency-coach buzzwords, "high-level operator" framing. The cohort is for guys in trucks. Talk like it.
  • The Alliance is the recurring layer and it stays. The cohort is not a replacement for the Alliance, it is the next rung. Alliance members get cohort priority application and a Standard-tier discount - protects retention rather than cannibalising it.
  • Jobber relationship is the load-bearing partnership. Anything that touches operator software stack mentions Jobber first. Do not propose a competing CRM.
  • No fabricated metrics about Keith's business. Stick to public claims: 40MM views, 250K weekly impressions, 2,500+ Academy students, Sterling Heights MI, 10K+ service businesses helped. Everything else stays at the level of plan, not promise.
  • The cohort teaches landscaping business operations. It does not guarantee revenue outcomes. That separation matters on every trades-coach surface.
