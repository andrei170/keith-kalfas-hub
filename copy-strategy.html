Foundation

Copy Strategy - Keith Kalfas

Foundation doc · Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, framing AG1 will write to.

Voice

Trades-register direct. Working-truck plain. Keith talks like a guy who has owned a landscaping route and a window-cleaning rig - because he has. The voice is the foreman, not the influencer. No agency-coach buzzwords. No high-level operator framing. No finance-bro jargon. The tone is "here is what is costing you on the next bid" - not "level up your potential."

Use: the bid, the route, the truck, windshield time, drop the price, raise the price, the crew, the foreman, the lead, the estimate, the route map, the per-property rate, the upsell, the trap, off the mower, owner-out-of-the-truck, your specific deal, install, the math Avoid: game-changer, unlock, transform, abundance, manifest, secrets, hacks, journey, vibe, disrupt, pivot, high-performer, level up your potential, financial freedom, passive income, "scale to seven figures" (unless paired with a specific revenue number from a public claim), 10x, blueprint (in body copy), grind (Keith's audience uses it differently than agency-coach Twitter)

Brand Pillars

  1. Trades-register, no buzzwords. The audience runs a truck. Talk like it. Every claim grounded in a concrete operator number - the bid, the route, the headcount, the hours-per-week off the mower.
  2. The Landscaping Employee Trap is the owned positioning phrase. Keith built the brand on that single phrase. Use it. The cohort solves the exit from the trap. The scorecard diagnoses which version of the trap is costing the operator the most right now.
  3. Operator, not agency-coach. Keith has done the work. He has run a route, fired a bad hire, raised a bid and lost the job, raised it again and won the next one. The voice never blurs that line. Avoid agency-coach syndrome where the language gets abstract.
  4. Implementation over inspiration. The Alliance has community. The courses have content. The cohort has installs - signed paperwork by the end of each module. The copy reflects what comes out the other side of the cohort, not what feeling the operator has during it.

Headline Bank

For the Landscaping Operator Cohort landing page, hub callouts, and ad creative:

  • The 8-week cohort for the operator who has watched every video and is still stuck on the mower.
  • Pricing. First crew lead. Route economics. Owner-out-of-the-truck plan. In that order.
  • The install version of the podcast - not another module library.
  • 20 seats per cohort. Hard cap. Weekly call. Real implementation.
  • Built for the operator past free content and not yet ready for a 1:1.
  • By week 4 your pricing is fixed. By week 8 your foreman is hired.
  • The cohort version of the call you can't book on the calendar.
  • From The Landscaping Employee Trap to the owner-out-of-the-truck plan.
  • The bid script, the interview script, the route map, the exit plan. In 8 weeks.
  • What gets installed in your business by the end of the cohort.

Framing for the Funnel

  • Top of funnel (scorecard, podcast end-cards, YouTube end-cards, IG hooks): operator-pain specific. "You are bidding $50/cut and burning out at $80K revenue" beats "level up your business." The scorecard surfaces which specific trap is currently costing the most.
  • Mid funnel (cohort landing, VSL, waitlist): the cohort is the install version of the podcast. The podcast tells you what to look for; the cohort walks your specific bid, your specific hire, your specific route, your specific exit plan.
  • High funnel (1:1 coaching call): the cohort grad who wants ongoing 1:1 books the call post-cohort. The 1:1 stays as the premium continuation, not the entry.

Banned Phrases

Universal AG1 ban list, plus Keith-specific: - No "blueprint" in body copy (it is fine in Keith's own existing product names like "Business Marketing Blueprint" because that is his trademark phrase - just do not use it as a verb / adjective in cohort copy) - No "secrets" or "hacks" - No "unlock" - No "transform" (unless paired with a concrete revenue or hours-off-the-mower number from a public student claim) - No "scale" as standalone - always followed by what specifically is being scaled (headcount, revenue, route density) - No "financial freedom" without a specific dollar number - No "shocking" or "the truth they don't want you to know" - against the brand voice - No "high-performer" / "high-level operator" framing - Keith's audience does not use that vocabulary about themselves - No "abundance" or "energy" or "mindset shift" as standalone - mindset language is fine when paired with a concrete decision (the bid Keith raised after the mindset shift, the hire he made after dropping the trap belief)

Voice Test

Every paragraph passes if a year-4 landscaping operator with 1 truck, 1 employee, $180K revenue and 60 windshield-hours per week nods, not rolls his eyes. If the copy could appear in a generic "build your business" course, rewrite it. If the copy includes a number, the number is operator-specific (not "thousands of dollars" - $1,200 per estimate, $45/hour windshield, $80K revenue stuck at the same number two years running).

Voice Test for Cohort Specifically

The cohort is mentorship, not a course. The copy must reflect that: - "Live group calls" not "video lessons" - "Office hours" not "Q&A bonus" - "20-25 seat cap" not "limited spots" - "Walk your specific bid" not "case studies" - The 8-week framing is structural, not aspirational ("by week 4 the first crew lead is hired," not "by the end you will feel transformed") - Pricing language is hint range only on the hub - never an exact dollar figure on cold surfaces

Voice Test for Trades-Register Specifically

  • Read every paragraph out loud as if Keith were saying it to a foreman in the cab of a truck. If a word would not get said in that context, cut it.
  • The reader is wearing work boots when he reads this page. The copy respects that.
  • Real numbers always - the $50 bid, the 3-truck operator, the $80K revenue ceiling, the year-4 wall - never "transformative" or "next-level"
  • The cohort solves The Landscaping Employee Trap - that is the owned, named, branded language. Use it.
