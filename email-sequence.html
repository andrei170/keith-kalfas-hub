Phase 1+2

Email Sequences - Keith Kalfas

Phase 1 + 2 deliverable · Pre-call nurture (3 touches), post-call (4 touches over 14 days), 14-day welcome flow.

Sequence 1 · Welcome Flow (14 days, 8 touches)

Triggered when a new subscriber finishes the Landscaping Business Trap Score. Segmented by stage (year-1 solo / 1-2 trucks / 3+ trucks / pre-revenue) and by trap type (pricing / hiring / route / owner-as-bottleneck).

T+0 - Immediate (Score Result)

Subject: Your Trap Score - [X/100] + the trap that's costing you the most right now Body: Score reveal, 1-paragraph context on what the score means against operators at the same stage, the specific trap surfaced from their answers, link to printable PDF action sheet, soft CTA toward the cohort waitlist (or Alliance / bundle depending on stage). Goal: deliver promise, set expectations, plant the cohort.

T+1 day - The Pricing Trap Story

Subject: The $50 bid that costs you $14K a year Body: Specific story on under-bidding. Walks the reader through the math on a real per-property job - the bid, the hours, the windshield time, the real per-hour rate. Closes with: "the cohort is the install version of this - we walk through your specific bid." Goal: prove implementation depth beyond a podcast episode.

T+3 days - The Hiring Trap Story

Subject: The crew lead I hired wrong - and what it cost me Body: Anonymised operator story on a bad hire. The job ad that was wrong, the interview script that missed it, the 30-day cost when the hire didn't stick. The fix. CTA → cohort waitlist. Goal: show what happens when the install does not exist.

T+5 days - The Route Economics Bridge

Subject: Your windshield-time-per-dollar is $14 and you don't know it yet Body: Routes the score-driven subscriber off pricing or hiring and into the route economics module of the cohort. Specific route example (one operator at $80K revenue, 60 windshield hours, the de-route plan that cut hours by 30%). CTA → cohort waitlist. Goal: broaden the cohort case beyond pricing.

T+8 days - The Owner-Out-Of-The-Truck Story

Subject: The 12-month plan to get off the mower Body: Personal story from Keith's voice. The 12-month sequence to remove the owner from production. The revenue checkpoint at month 3, month 6, month 12. The specific decisions at each stage. CTA → cohort waitlist. Goal: prove the cohort spans the full operator lift, not just one module.

T+11 days - The Cohort Application Open

Subject: Cohort applications open - 20 to 25 seats, 8 weeks Body: Direct cohort pitch. Format, cap, 8-week structure, what gets installed by week 4 vs week 8, the office-hours cadence, the Alliance retention discount, the 1:1-as-continuation framing. CTA → application Typeform. Goal: convert the warm subscriber to the application.

T+13 days - Soft Pitch Open

Subject: Quick question before I disappear from your inbox Body: Two-line check-in. "Is the cohort a fit for where you're at?" Application link. "Or hit reply and tell me which trap is on your desk this month - pricing, hiring, route, or getting off the mower." Goal: segment hot vs warm; route hot to application, warm to a 1:1 reply for triage.

T+14 days - Last Touch (segmented)

  • Year-1 solo / pre-revenue: "If you are just starting out, the YouTube long-form series is free and a better fit than the cohort. Start there." → routes to flagship YouTube link.
  • 1-2 truck operator (Hot): Last call for cohort cycle. CTA → application.
  • 3+ truck operator: "If you are past three trucks and need 1:1 narrow help, the coaching call is the right fit - cohort is for the 1-2 truck stage." → routes to keithkalfas.com/call.
  • Not a fit yet: "Stay on the list - I send the next score-card update when a new trap shows up in the data." → tags as long-term nurture.

Sequence 2 · Pre-Call Nurture (3 touches before cohort intro call)

Triggered when a Landscaping Operator Cohort applicant books the optional intro call (or when someone books a 1:1 coaching call - same logic, different copy).

T-7 days

Subject: Before our call - 3 questions I'll ask you Body: The three questions Keith will ask, in advance. Helps the applicant arrive prepared. (Q1: what is your current annual revenue and how long stuck at that number? Q2: how many windshield hours per week are you running? Q3: which trap is costing you the most right now - pricing, hiring, route, or owner-as-bottleneck?)

T-3 days

Subject: What I learned working with [N] cohort operators last cycle Body: Story-driven. One specific "before-call" insight from the prior cohort. Sets the tone.

T-1 day

Subject: Tomorrow at [time] - link inside, 1 thing to bring Body: Calendar reminder, one specific prep ask (the operator's last 3 estimates, the current crew roster, or the windshield-hours-per-week count - whichever fits the call topic).

Sequence 3 · Post-Call Follow-Up (4 touches over 14 days)

Triggered after the cohort intro call (or post-1:1 call) regardless of outcome.

T+1 hour - Recap

Subject: Recap from our call + 2 next steps Body: 1-paragraph recap, 2 specific action items Keith committed to, link to the agreed-upon path (cohort enrolment / 1:1 continuation / Alliance / wait list).

T+3 days - The Specific Resource

Subject: That [thing] I mentioned on the call Body: Whatever specific resource Keith promised (bid script template, interview script, route map worksheet, owner-out-of-truck planner). Sent as clean PDF or shared Notion.

T+7 days - The Check-In

Subject: Quick - did you run [X] yet? Body: Soft accountability nudge. "Reply with the number if you did. Reply with a question if you didn't."

T+14 days - The Decision

Subject: Where you landed Body: Clean ask: "Are we moving forward with the cohort or holding off?" No pressure. Clear decision frame. Either route triggers the right next sequence (onboarding or long-term nurture).

Subject Line Bank (rotation)

For ongoing broadcasts beyond the welcome sequence:

  • "The bid I would not have written 5 years ago"
  • "Three numbers I look at before I take on a new property"
  • "The crew lead pay structure that finally stuck"
  • "Why I cut my route by 18 stops this season"
  • "The owner-out-of-the-truck checkpoint at month 6"
  • "The cohort question I keep getting asked"
  • "The Alliance call last week - the trap that came up 4 times"
  • "What I told the operator on the 1:1 last Tuesday"
  • "The Untrapped Podcast clip I keep getting DMs about"

Triggers + Tags

  • Tag by score band on Trap Score completion (0-40 / 41-70 / 71-100)
  • Tag by stage (year-1 solo / 1-2 trucks / 3+ trucks / pre-revenue)
  • Tag by trap type (pricing / hiring / route / owner-as-bottleneck)
  • Tag by purchase (Cohort Standard / Cohort Premium / 1:1 booked / Alliance member / bundle purchaser)
  • Tag by content engagement (opened 5+ in 30 days = "warm subscriber")
  • Re-engagement campaign at 60 days of inactivity
  • Auto-suppress promotional during cohort live weeks (replace with cohort-only track for actives)
  • Alliance member tag triggers cohort-priority-application broadcast 14 days before next cohort opens
