Three Things I'd Build Differently - Keith Kalfas

Spent the morning mapping your funnel. The free content is the strongest in the niche. The Alliance is the recurring layer. The $97-$347 courses sit underneath. The 1:1 coaching call sits on top. What is missing is the $2K-$5K cohort layer between the Alliance and the 1:1 call - the structured group product for the operator who has already paid for the bundle and wants implementation help, not another module library. All three install in less than 7 days from kickoff.

Snapshot

  • 36K IG @keithkalfas + TikTok mirror + The Untrapped Podcast catalogue + YouTube long-form base (former "Landscaping Employee Trap" channel)
  • Stated lifetime reach: 40MM video views · 250K weekly social impressions · 2,500+ Academy students · 10K+ service businesses helped (public claims)
  • Authored 4 books in the niche · Jobber Ambassador · annual Marketing ROI live event in Michigan
  • Free content + lead magnets (PDF + workshop) at the top
  • Course bundle ($347) and individual courses ($97-$147) in the low-ticket band

Thing #1 - The mid-tier cohort layer between the Alliance and the 1:1 coaching call

The asymmetry: The Alliance does the community + recurring-revenue job at $97/mo. The courses do the low-ticket job at $97-$347. The 1:1 coaching call does the high-touch job at the top. There is no $2K-$5K structured group product in between. An operator who has already paid for the bundle, listens to the podcast in the truck, and is now stuck at $80K-$200K revenue trying to hire and price his way past the ceiling has two options: pay $97/mo for the Alliance (community, not structured) or book a 1:1 (capped by your calendar). Neither is the right shape for that operator.

A "Landscaping Operator Cohort" - 8 weeks, weekly group call with Keith, scheduled office hours, 20-25 seat cap - built around four installs that an operator can sign their name to:

  • Pricing fixed (week 1-2) - the bid script, the per-property pricing matrix, the upsell ladder, raised on the next 3 estimates the operator runs during the cohort
  • First crew lead hired (week 3-4) - the job ad, the interview script, the pay structure, the 30-60-90 onboarding plan
  • Route economics locked (week 5-6) - the route map, the windshield-time-per-dollar number, the de-route plan
  • Owner-out-of-the-truck plan (week 7-8) - the 12-month sequence to remove the owner from production, with the dollar checkpoint at each stage

Why this pencils:

  • 20-25 seats per cohort x $2K-$5K = $40K-$125K per cohort
  • 4-6 cohorts per year = $160K-$750K direct revenue
  • Time cost on you: weekly group call (60-75 min) + 2x monthly office-hours blocks. Not weekly content production. Not 1:1 hourly.
  • AG1 builds the funnel layer, the landing page, the application Typeform, the cohort onboarding flow, the pre-call and post-call emails - so the marketing surface time-cost on you is zero past Day 1 filming
  • Alliance retention lifts (cohort grads stay on as Alliance members at the higher tier - the cohort is the upgrade path, not the off-ramp)
  • The 1:1 coaching slot stays as the high-touch continuation for cohort grads who want ongoing - protected, not replaced

Cost of waiting: Each quarter the cohort does not exist is 1-2 cycles of $40K-$125K not collected. The 36K IG audience plus the podcast catalogue is hot right now. By the time the cohort launches next year instead of this year, the same audience has either bought the bundle (low-ticket exit) or paid for a 1:1 (your-hour exit). The mid-tier captures the in-between buyer that the current funnel loses.

The fix: - "Landscaping Operator Cohort" - 8 weeks, 20-25 seat cap - Modules: pricing (week 1-2), first crew lead (week 3-4), route economics (week 5-6), owner-out-of-truck plan (week 7-8) - Weekly group call (60-75 min), 2x monthly office-hours, private community channel (Skool or Circle) - Mid-tier standard tier, premium tier adds a 1:1 business audit call and 3 follow-up 30-min check-ins in the 90 days post-cohort - Cohort grads get a Standard-tier discount to renew on the Alliance + first-look on the next cohort

Estimated impact: Year-1 floor $160K-$600K direct on the cohort line, plus Alliance retention lift (cohort grads continue as members), plus 1:1 spillover from cohort grads booking ongoing.

Why this is #1: It is the product that the existing audience size already justifies. The math is mentorship-shaped, the time cost is bounded, and it is the only layer in the funnel that does not yet exist.

Thing #2 - 36K IG plus a podcast catalogue, no scorecard capturing email

The leak: The lead magnets are PDFs ("7 Steps to Marketing Your Business," "Free Mini Marketing course," "Brand Advantage Workshop"). PDFs capture an email and deliver one piece of content. They do not diagnose. A year-one solo landscaper bidding $50/cut and a year-five operator running 3 trucks at $1M revenue both download the same PDF. The list cannot tell them apart. The next email goes to both. The product recommendation goes to both. The conversion math on every downstream offer pays for the lowest common denominator.

A "Landscaping Business Trap Score" - 12 questions, returns a 0-100 score plus a 3-line bottleneck readout (which trap is currently costing you the most: pricing, hiring, route economics, or owner-as-bottleneck) - captures email, segments by stage, and routes the subscriber to the right next product (Alliance for community-stage, bundle for content-stage, cohort for implementation-stage, 1:1 call for top-stage).

Why it's costing you:

  • 5-8% IG-to-email conversion is realistic on a high-intent trades audience with the right diagnostic = 2K-3K incremental subscribers in 90 days from the existing IG base alone, 5K-8K in 6 months once the podcast end-card + YouTube end-card route to the scorecard
  • Without segmentation, the cohort waitlist and the 1:1 page are pitched to subscribers who should be on the $97/mo Alliance, and the Alliance is pitched to subscribers who are already past it and should be in the cohort. Conversion math gets compressed at every tier.
  • The PDF capture has been the same shape for years. The scorecard is the version that compounds with every new follower instead of paying for one PDF download

Cost of waiting: Every month without the scorecard, the IG and podcast audience grows but the email list does not segment. By the time the cohort opens, there is no warm pre-segmented list to launch to. The launch is harder, the cost-per-seat for cohort cycle 1 is higher, and the dependency on cold paid acquisition rises.

The fix: - "Landscaping Business Trap Score" - 12 questions on ScoreApp or Typeform Logic - Returns score (0-100) + 3-line bottleneck readout + custom 1-page PDF action sheet - Captures email, segments by stage (year-1 solo / 1-2 trucks / 3+ trucks / pre-revenue) and by trap type (pricing / hiring / route / owner-as-bottleneck) - Triggers 7-touch segmented nurture sequence routed by segment + trap

Estimated impact: Year-1 lift $60K-$160K incremental from scorecard-segmented sales across the existing product ladder. Compounds for life of the list.

Why this is #2: Foundational for #1 and #3. Fast install, less than 7 days. The compounding asset every other layer rides on. The scorecard tells you which subscriber is cohort-ready, which is Alliance-ready, and which still needs the bundle - so the right offer goes to the right inbox.

Thing #3 - The Untrapped Podcast catalogue has no productised cohort waitlist or end-card upsell

The contradiction: The podcast is on every major platform and has hundreds of episodes. Each episode is 30-60 minutes of listener time with Keith already in their ears. That is the warmest possible audience for a $2K-$5K cohort - they are not casual scrollers, they are operators who put a podcast on in the truck and listened to the whole thing. Right now the end-card on each episode is either no CTA, a generic "go to keithkalfas.com," or an affiliate plug. The cohort that does not exist yet (#1) is the natural product for that listener. The waitlist page is the asset that captures them between cohorts.

A "Cohort Waitlist" page - one-pager with the cohort overview, application form, cohort cycle dates, what gets installed by week 4 vs week 8 - sits at one short URL. The podcast end-card, the YouTube end-card, the IG bio router, and the podcast show notes all route here. Application is open year-round. Cohort cycles run quarterly. The Alliance stays as the recurring layer; the waitlist is the implementation upgrade for listeners who want to do real work between Alliance calls.

Why it's costing you:

  • The podcast catalogue is the warmest top-of-funnel asset and is currently routing to nothing productised above the $347 bundle
  • Each new episode shipped without the waitlist link in the end-card is a leaked conversion - the listener has already invested 30-60 minutes of attention, then has nowhere structured to land
  • The YouTube long-form catalogue has the same problem at scale - 10+ years of evergreen "how to start a landscaping business" content with no end-card route to a mid-tier mentorship

Cost of waiting: Each month the waitlist does not exist, the podcast and YouTube catalogue continues to route warm listeners to either the $97/mo Alliance (under-sells the high-intent listener) or the 1:1 coaching call (capped by your hours). The mid-tier listener has nowhere to land. The compounding lift on a single end-card swap is real and currently unmeasured.

The fix: - "Cohort Waitlist" one-page hub with application form (4-minute Typeform) - Cohort overview + cycle dates + what gets installed by week 4 vs week 8 - Podcast end-card baked into next 10 episodes ("if this episode landed, the cohort is the install version - link in the show notes") - YouTube end-card update on flagship videos with same routing - IG bio link router updated to add the cohort waitlist as a primary option - Podcast show-notes template updated to add the waitlist link

Estimated impact: Year-1 floor $80K-$200K incremental from podcast and YouTube attributable cohort applications. Compounds with every new episode shipped.

Why this is #3: Smaller ceiling than #1, dependent on #1 existing (the waitlist routes to the cohort), and dependent on #2 for the email-capture infrastructure. But it locks the long-form catalogue into the funnel for life. Every future episode and every YouTube long-form is now a working top-of-funnel asset for the cohort.
